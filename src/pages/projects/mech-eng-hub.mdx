# The Mechanical Engineering Hub

Opinionated collection of websites, software, and guides for Mechanical Engineers. Quality over quantity.

> Inspired by [awesome-mecheng](https://github.com/m2n037/awesome-mecheng?tab=readme-ov-file#for-cad). The goal: the best of the best, kept current as I learn more.

## Introductory General Readings

1. [How to read a book](https://fs.blog/how-to-read-a-book/)
2. [How to read a research paper](http://ccr.sigcomm.org/online/files/p83-keshavA.pdf)
3. [How complex systems fail](https://how.complexsystems.fail/)

## Introductory Reading for Math & Physics

1. [The Feynman Lectures on Physics](https://www.feynmanlectures.caltech.edu/)
2. [Calculus Made Easy](https://calculusmadeeasy.org/)

## Textbooks & Research Papers Resources

1. [Library Genesis](https://libgen.is/) — vast library of books.
2. [Sci-Hub](https://sci-hub.se/) — access to scientific papers.
   - [Sci-Hub X Now!](https://github.com/gchenfc/sci-hub-now)
   - [Zotero Scihub](https://github.com/ethanwillis/zotero-scihub)
3. [Open Source Mechanical Engineering Books](https://www.intechopen.com/subjects/121)
4. [Free Computer Books](https://freecomputerbooks.com/)

> To use Sci-Hub, grab a DOI or URL (find via [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/)).
